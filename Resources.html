Denver Health School-Based Health Centers

Denver Healthâ€™s School-Based Health Centers (SBHCs) offers specialized pediatric focused care to treat most health conditions that affect schoolaged children. Services are available throughout the school year to any Denver Public Schools (DPS) student or child that attends a DPS affiliated Early Childhood Education (ECE) center within Denver County. SBHCs offer convenient care that limits the amount of time students are out of class and parent/guardians have to be off work. In many cases, students are able to get same day or next day appointments.


Shots for Tots and Teens

Shots for Tots and Teens' goal is to make sure every child receives the immunizations needed to keep them healthy.

The program offers low- and no-cost Saturday immunization clinics throughout the year in Aurora, Denver and Arvada with the help of experienced immunization nurses, fire medics, paramedics, health care and community volunteers. Each clinic also offers fun activities and prizes for kids, including fire engine and ambulance tours.


Clothes to Kids Denver

The mission of Clothes To Kids of Denver is to provide new and quality used clothing to students from low-income or in-crisis families in the Denver Metro Area, free of charge. Clothes To Kids is not a consignment store and distributes all of the items that are donated to students in the community for free. Students attending Farrell B. Howell School automatically qualify to select a free school wardrobe from Clothes To Kids of Denver.

***Students need an appointment to shop, even if they automatically qualify for services. Please call 720-379-4630, ext. 2 to schedule an appointment.

The following forms of proof of enrollment are accepted:

  • Student ID
  • Copy of Student Record
  • Student Report Card
  • Free and Reduced Lunch Letter
  • Letter on school letterhead showing proof of enrollment
